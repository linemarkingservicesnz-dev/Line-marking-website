BLOG POST 1: HOW LONG DOES CAR PARK LINE MARKING LAST?
====================================================

TITLE: How Long Does Car Park Line Marking Last in New Zealand?
CATEGORY: Line Marking | DATE: March 2025

One of the most common questions we get from facilities managers and property
owners is: how long will my line marking actually last? The honest answer
depends on several factors — but understanding them helps you plan maintenance,
budget correctly, and avoid the safety risks that come with faded markings.

TYPICAL LIFESPAN: 18 TO 24 MONTHS
Under normal conditions, car park line marking lasts between 18 and 24 months.
This applies to standard road paint applied to asphalt or concrete surfaces in
a typical commercial or industrial setting. After this point, lines begin to
fade and lose visibility — which creates both safety issues and compliance risks.

WHAT AFFECTS HOW LONG MARKINGS LAST:
- Traffic volume — High-traffic car parks wear faster. Busy sites may need
  re-marking every 6 to 12 months.
- Surface type — Concrete holds paint longer than asphalt.
- Paint or resin quality — NZTA-approved paints last significantly longer.
  Epoxy and polyurethane resins outlast standard paints by years.
- UV exposure — Outdoor markings fade faster due to NZ's high UV index.
- Surface preparation — Properly cleaned and primed surfaces hold paint far better.
- Weather — Freeze-thaw cycles, heavy rain, and heat all affect longevity.

WHEN SHOULD YOU RE-MARK:
- Schedule a re-mark when lines are noticeably faded but still visible.
- Include line marking in your annual site safety review.
- High-traffic areas like loading zones and pedestrian crossings should be
  checked every 6 months.

CHOOSING THE RIGHT MATERIAL:
- Epoxy resin — Ideal for indoor concrete floors. Extremely durable and
  chemical-resistant.
- Polyurethane — Best for outdoor surfaces. UV-resistant and flexible,
  ideal for New Zealand's climate.
- MMA resin — Fast-setting and extremely hard-wearing. Best for high-traffic
  outdoor areas.

BLOG POST 2: EPOXY VS POLYURETHANE FLOOR COATINGS
====================================================

TITLE: Epoxy vs Polyurethane Floor Coatings: Which is Right for Your Warehouse?
CATEGORY: Flooring | DATE: February 2025

When it comes to industrial floor coatings, two products dominate: epoxy and
polyurethane. Choosing the wrong one can mean premature failure, costly repairs,
and unnecessary downtime.

WHAT IS EPOXY FLOORING:
- Exceptional hardness and impact resistance
- Highly resistant to chemicals, oils, and solvents
- Easy to clean and maintain
- Can be applied with non-slip aggregates for safety
- Excellent adhesion to concrete when properly prepared

WHAT IS POLYURETHANE FLOORING:
- UV-resistant — does not yellow or degrade in sunlight
- More flexible, handles thermal movement better
- Better resistance to abrasion over time
- Suitable for outdoor and exposed areas
- Resistant to staining and chemical attack

EPOXY: BEST FOR:
- Warehouses and distribution centres
- Manufacturing plant floors
- Workshops and garages
- Commercial kitchens (with food-safe epoxy)
- Any indoor high-traffic concrete floor

POLYURETHANE: BEST FOR:
- Outdoor yards and loading areas
- Car parks with sun exposure
- Areas subject to temperature fluctuation
- Facilities in coastal or high-UV environments
- As a topcoat over epoxy for enhanced protection

CAN YOU USE BOTH TOGETHER:
Yes. A common approach is to apply epoxy as a base coat for its hardness and
adhesion, then top it with polyurethane for UV resistance and abrasion
protection. This combination is particularly effective for outdoor or semi-
exposed areas.

COST COMPARISON:
Epoxy is generally more affordable than polyurethane as a standalone product.
However, polyurethane often delivers better long-term value in outdoor settings
when you factor in longevity and reapplication costs.

BLOG POST 3: WAREHOUSE PEDESTRIAN WALKWAY MARKINGS
====================================================

TITLE: Why Every Warehouse Needs Clear Pedestrian Walkway Markings
CATEGORY: Safety | DATE: January 2025

Warehouses are one of the highest-risk work environments in New Zealand. Forklifts,
pallet jacks, and heavy machinery share the same space as workers on foot — and
when the movement of people and vehicles isn't clearly defined, accidents happen.

THE RISK:
WorkSafe New Zealand consistently identifies pedestrian/vehicle interaction as
a leading cause of serious harm in warehouses and distribution centres.

WHAT THE LAW SAYS:
Under the Health and Safety at Work Act 2015, employers have a duty to manage
risks in the workplace. For warehouses and factories, this includes:
- Separating pedestrian and vehicle traffic where practicable
- Clearly marking safe walking routes for workers
- Ensuring visitors and contractors can safely navigate your site
- Maintaining markings so they remain clear and visible

WHAT GOOD WALKWAY MARKING LOOKS LIKE:
- Pedestrian corridors at least 1 metre wide
- Forklift lanes marked separately from pedestrian zones
- Zebra crossing markings at vehicle crossing points
- Hazard zones around blind corners, loading docks, and intersections
- Keep-clear zones in front of emergency exits and fire equipment
- High-visibility colours (yellow for pedestrian zones is common practice)

THE RIGHT PAINT FOR WAREHOUSE FLOORS:
- Epoxy — Bonds to concrete, extremely durable, chemical-resistant
- Polyurethane — More flexible, good for temperature fluctuation areas
- Non-slip aggregates — Can be added for wet or high-risk areas

HOW OFTEN SHOULD WAREHOUSE MARKINGS BE REFRESHED:
- Annual inspection of all floor markings
- Immediate repainting of any faded or damaged markings
- Proactive scheduling of re-marking during shutdowns or quiet periods

BLOG POST 4: PAINTED VS THERMOPLASTIC PLAYGROUND MARKINGS
====================================================

TITLE: Painted vs Thermoplastic Playground Markings: What's Better for NZ Schools?
CATEGORY: Playground | DATE: December 2024

If you're planning new playground markings for your school, you've likely come
across two main options: painted markings or thermoplastic stickers. For most
New Zealand primary schools, painted markings offer better value, safety, and
longevity.

THERMOPLASTIC MARKINGS — DRAWBACKS:
- Sit on top of the surface, creating a raised edge — a trip hazard
- Edges lift over time, particularly with temperature fluctuation
- Once lifted, they can peel and leave adhesive residue
- Significantly more expensive than painted alternatives
- Replacement requires removing the old thermoplastic first

PAINTED PLAYGROUND MARKINGS — ADVANTAGES:
- Completely flush with the surface — no trip hazards
- More affordable upfront and much cheaper to refresh
- Fully customisable — any design, size, colour, or school theme
- Easier to modify as the school's needs change
- Can include non-slip properties for wet conditions
- Designed to withstand NZ's UV, rain, and temperature variations

WHICH LASTS LONGER:
Painted markings often outlast thermoplastic in high-activity areas. Once
thermoplastic starts to lift — which happens in NZ's climate — it deteriorates
quickly. Painted markings simply fade gradually and can be refreshed with a
top coat without any removal required.

COST COMPARISON:
Thermoplastic typically costs 3-5 times more than equivalent painted markings.

WHAT WE RECOMMEND FOR NZ SCHOOLS:
Painted markings for playground games — safer, more affordable, more
customisable, and easier to maintain.

BLOG POST 5: HOW TO PREPARE FOR LINE MARKING
====================================================

TITLE: How to Prepare Your Site for Line Marking — and Why It Matters
CATEGORY: Tips | DATE: November 2024

Good preparation is the key to long-lasting line marking. The surface condition
before we arrive is the single most important factor in how long your lines last.

WHY PREPARATION MATTERS:
Paint needs a clean, dry, sound surface to bond properly. Contaminants like
oil, grease, dust, rubber, and moisture all reduce adhesion — meaning markings
can lift, fade, or peel much earlier than they should. Good prep can be the
difference between markings lasting 12 months and markings lasting 3 years.

FOR CAR PARKS — WHAT WE NEED FROM YOU:
- Surface is dry — no rain for 24 hours before the job.
- Area is cleared of vehicles — all cars moved out before we begin.
- Oil stains are treated — heavy oil deposits need to be cleaned first.
- Existing markings to be removed are flagged — we can black out or grind old lines.
- You've approved a layout plan — for new markings, we provide a plan beforehand.

FOR WAREHOUSES — KEY CONSIDERATIONS:
- Floor must be clean and free of oils, chemicals, and dust
- Damaged concrete should be repaired before coating is applied
- Area being marked should be free of machinery and stock
- For epoxy coatings, we typically need to grind or acid-etch the concrete
- Curing time must be factored in — some coatings require 24-48 hours

WHAT WE HANDLE:
- Surface cleaning and preparation (if included in scope)
- Measuring and layout before any paint is applied
- Masking and taping for clean, sharp lines
- Application using professional line marking equipment
- Clean-up and removal of all masking materials

SCHEDULING:
We offer after-hours and weekend scheduling at standard weekday rates — no
premium for working around your hours.
